## Hook Types

Classify each title/cover into one primary hook:

- direct benefit: clear outcome, such as low sugar, high protein, fast breakfast
- pain point: solves texture, calories, ingredients, satiety, bloating, price, storage
- curiosity gap: "why nobody talks about this", "I regret finding this late"
- comparison: almond butter versus peanut butter, homemade versus bought, brand A versus brand B
- recipe/use case: toast, oatmeal, yogurt bowl, latte, baking, dipping sauce
- identity: fitness, vegan, clean eating, office worker, student, mom, dessert lover
- mistake avoidance: buying guide, ingredient traps, calories, sugar alcohols, oxidation
- listicle: several eating methods, brands, pairings, breakfast ideas

## Visual Patterns

For each note, inspect the first image and carousel:

- Is the product visible in the first viewport?
- Does the cover show texture, spoon pull, spread, recipe result, label, calorie panel, or comparison?
- Is cover text short enough to read on mobile?
- Does the carousel progress from result -> proof -> method -> product -> CTA?
- Are colors food-natural, clean fitness, lifestyle desk, grocery shelf, or recipe kitchen?

## Analysis Output

Produce:

1. Top notes table with metrics and source links.
2. Pattern frequency table: hook type, visual pattern, scene, audience, content angle.
3. 5-10 reusable formulas, each grounded in at least 2 observed notes.
4. Specific opportunities for new notes:
   - title templates
   - cover image plan
   - carousel page order
   - body outline
   - comment bait/question
   - product proof to capture

Avoid claiming causal certainty. Phrase insights as observed patterns unless controlled testing data is available.
