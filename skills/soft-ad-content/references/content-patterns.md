# Content Patterns

Use this reference when generating product soft ads from content teardowns.

## Mechanism Extraction

Convert the user's breakdown into this compact brief before writing:

- Audience: who urgently cares.
- Trigger: what made them stop scrolling.
- Pain: what problem or desire was activated.
- Promise: what relief, transformation, or insight was implied.
- Proof: what made it believable.
- Rhythm: how the content moved from hook to payoff.
- Product bridge: where the product naturally belongs.

## Product Bridge Types

- Scene bridge: "I ran into this exact situation, and this product was part of the fix."
- Habit bridge: "The product is a small repeatable action inside a broader routine."
- Proof bridge: "The product's feature demonstrates the post's main claim."
- Comparison bridge: "The product helps explain what to choose or avoid."
- Mistake bridge: "The user failed before because they lacked this product, method, or perspective."
- Comment bridge: "The product appears in replies after readers ask how to do it."

## Xiaohongshu Note Structure

Include:

- 5-8 title options with different angles.
- 3 cover text options under 16 Chinese characters when writing in Chinese, or under 8 words when writing in English.
- Opening: pain, identity, or surprising observation.
- Body: 3-5 short sections with lived detail.
- Product insertion: one natural paragraph or bullet after the pain is clear.
- CTA: save, comment, ask, compare, or try a low-friction action.
- Comment prompts: 5-8 seeded comments, including skeptical, curious, confirming, and detail-seeking voices.

Prefer sensory and situational detail over slogans.

## Short-Video Structure

Use this sequence:

1. 0-3s hook: interruption, contradiction, result, or identity callout.
2. 3-8s problem scene: show the audience's current frustration.
3. 8-18s insight: explain what most people miss.
4. 18-35s product bridge: show the product solving one concrete part of the problem.
5. 35-45s proof or micro-result: demonstrate, compare, or summarize.
6. Ending CTA: ask for comment, save, DM keyword, or next-step action.

Provide shot suggestions only when useful for filming.

## Route Variations

Generate varied directions by changing one lever at a time:

- Hook: fear, curiosity, identity, checklist, result, myth-busting, story.
- Role: expert, friend, beginner, reviewer, customer, founder, observer.
- Proof: before/after, checklist, scenario, quote, demo, comparison, repeated use.
- CTA: comment keyword, save checklist, ask for model, share to a friend, try first step.

## Soft-Ad Self-Check

Before final output, verify:

- The product appears after the audience problem is emotionally or practically established.
- The product's value is concrete: feature, scenario, proof, or outcome.
- The draft could plausibly exist as normal platform content.
- The CTA does not break the native tone.
- Any claims are supportable from user-provided product facts.
