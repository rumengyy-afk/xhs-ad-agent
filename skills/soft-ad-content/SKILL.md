# Soft Ad Content

## Overview

Turn a content breakdown into product-fit soft ads by preserving the original content's winning mechanism, then weaving the product into the user's pain point, scenario, proof, and decision moment.

If the request involves Xiaohongshu/XHS notes, Douyin/TikTok short-video scripts, seeded social content, native ads, or product placement based on a previous content teardown, use this skill.

## Inputs To Collect

Work with whatever the user provides. If key context is missing, make reasonable assumptions and label them briefly.

Prioritize these inputs:

- Content breakdown: hook, target audience, pain point, conflict, rhythm, structure, comment insight, title/cover logic, conversion moment.
- Product context: product name, category, target user, core benefit, differentiator, price/offer, proof, usage scenario, constraints.
- Platform and format: Xiaohongshu/XHS image-text note, Douyin/TikTok short video, Bilibili, WeChat article, WeChat Moments, comment seeding, or ad script.
- Desired tone: personal experience, expert recommendation, story, review, checklist, comparison, anti-hard-sell, emotional resonance.
- Compliance constraints: claims to avoid, regulated category limits, required disclaimers, banned words, brand voice.

## Workflow

1. Extract the viral mechanism from the breakdown.
   Identify why the original content worked: curiosity gap, identity resonance, fear relief, transformation, practicality, controversy, social proof, or saved effort.

2. Map the product to the mechanism.
   Connect the product to the same emotional or practical job. Avoid forcing the product into the hook before the audience's problem feels real.

3. Choose the soft-ad role.
   Pick one primary role for the product:
   - Solution after pain is established
   - Tool used inside a real scene
   - Proof that supports the author's conclusion
   - Small habit or upgrade in a broader story
   - Comparison object that clarifies the user's choice
   - Comment-triggered recommendation

4. Draft native content.
   Match the platform's language, pacing, and structure. Keep the ad reveal late enough to feel earned, but clear enough that the reader understands the product's value.

5. Add conversion assets.
   Include titles, opening hooks, body/script, product insertion line, CTA, comment prompts, and optional visual/shot suggestions when relevant.

6. Self-check before finalizing.
   Remove exaggerated claims, generic hype, stiff brand language, and salesy transitions like "today I recommend." Make the product feel like part of the story, not a pasted sponsor block.

## Output Formats

When the user does not specify a format, produce 3 routes:

- Native note: title options, cover text, body, CTA, comment prompt.
- Short-video script: hook, scene sequence, voiceover, product insertion, closing CTA.
- Seeded comment set: 5-8 comments from different user perspectives.

For each route, explain in one short sentence which part of the original breakdown it borrows.

## Quality Bar

Make the output:

- Specific to the product and audience, not generic marketing copy.
- Soft but still commercially useful.
- Platform-native, with natural phrasing and realistic user detail.
- Built from the user's content teardown rather than an unrelated ad idea.
- Honest about assumptions and claim limits.

Avoid:

- Medical, financial, or guaranteed-result claims unless the user provides compliant proof.
- Fake personal experience stated as fact. Use phrasing like "write this as..." or draft as a script if experience is fictional.
- Directly copying another creator's wording beyond short structural inspiration.
- Overusing buzzwords, emojis, hashtags, or forced internet slang.
